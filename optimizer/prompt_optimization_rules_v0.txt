Prompt optimization rules for image-editing VLM QC:

1. Optimize for Precision first. FP means human fail but model pass, and is the highest-risk error for training-data filtering.
2. Prefer reducing recurring FP patterns over improving aggregate Accuracy.
3. Do not accept a prompt change that creates a large FN increase without an explicit trade-off.
4. Modify prompts only for visible, recurring, generalizable failures.
5. Do not overfit one sample or add a long one-case checklist.
6. Preserve tolerance for invisible, occluded, cropped, blurry, tiny, shadowed, or ambiguous regions.
7. For texture transfer, fail clear target residue, non-target contamination, and garment structural anchor changes.
8. For texture transfer, structural anchors include neckline, collar, cuffs, sleeve length, hem, zipper, buttons, cutouts, straps, laces, pockets, hood, openings, silhouette, fit, and layering.
9. Save each accepted prompt as a new version. Do not overwrite previous prompt versions.
10. After writing a new version, re-run evaluation and compare Precision, Recall, FP, FN, and F1.
